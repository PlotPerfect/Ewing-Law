October 10, 2018
Ewing Law at The Boxing Writer’s Awards

On Monday 8th October 2018 Ewing Law was privileged to host a table at this year’s Boxing Writer’s Club’s Awards dinner, held at The Savoy Hotel in London.

At over 100 years old, The Boxing Writer’s Club is the sport’s oldest association and its awards are the crown jewel in the British boxing calendar. We are particularly proud, then, that for the second year running Ewing Law were the only ‘non-boxing’ organisation invited to host a table of its own.

Along with Scott Ewing, Tony Wyatt and Tony Vieira, our guests spent the night mingling with heroes and legends of the British ring in a night they will never forget. And to cap it all, Scott Ewing was personally named in the Roll Call of Honour – usually reserved for the many champions in the room – for his unending legal services to boxing and to boxers.

It was a truly amazing night.

October 13, 2018
The Float Like A Butterfly Ball 2018

Tonight is the night!!

We are now just hours away from the biggest charity event of the year, The Float Like A Butterfly Ball in aid of the amazing Caudwell Children.

As former boxers, Scott Ewing and Tony Wyatt remain passionate about their sport and with The Float Like A Butterfly Ball they devised a way to use that passion to raise money for sick and needy children.

First held in 2017 where the night raised almost £170,000, The Float Like A Butterfly Ball is a prestige night of white collar boxing and first-class entertainment, held in the Great Room of The Grosvenor House Hotel on Park Lane and filled with the rich and the famous.

Hosted by Kirsty Gallagher, former World Middleweight and World SuperMiddleweight Champion Steve Collins acting as referee for four bouts – and with former World Cruiserweight Champion Johnny Nelson manning the mic for the live commentary – and with amazing live musical acts throughout the night, the event promises to raise funds in the best possible way: via a truly great night out!

Ewing Law is proud to be the hosts of this amazing event, and to continue to support Caudwell Children, a truly incredible charity doing the most vital work imaginable.
